Analyze the genealogical source document: $ARGUMENTS

Load the `gps-methodology`, `citation-guide`, and `record-types` skills for reference.

**Step 1 — Identify the source.**
If a file was provided, read it. If an image was uploaded, examine it. If the researcher describes a source verbally, work with their description.

Determine:
- What type of record this is
- When it was created
- Who created it
- What jurisdiction or institution it came from

**Step 2 — Transcribe (if applicable).**
If the source is a handwritten document or image, provide a verbatim transcription. Preserve original spelling, punctuation, and capitalization. Mark illegible words as [illegible] and uncertain readings as [unclear: possible word].

**Step 3 — Extract genealogical information.**
Pull out every piece of genealogically relevant information:
- Names (including variants, maiden names, aliases)
- Dates (birth, death, marriage, other events)
- Places (as specific as possible)
- Relationships stated or implied
- Occupations, ages, and other personal details
- Informant identity (who provided the information on this record)

**Step 4 — Classify the source.**
Apply GPS classification:
- **Source type**: Original record, derivative, or authored narrative?
- **Information type**: Primary (firsthand), secondary (secondhand), or undetermined?
- **Evidence type**: Direct (explicitly states the fact), indirect (requires inference), or negative (expected info absent)?

Explain each classification in plain language so the researcher understands why it matters.

**Step 5 — Identify what this source proves and what it does not.**
State clearly:
- What facts this source directly establishes
- What facts it suggests but does not prove
- What questions it raises
- What other sources should be checked to corroborate or clarify

**Step 6 — Trigger citation coaching.**
After analysis, tell the researcher: "Now let's cite this source properly so you can find it again and anyone can verify your work."

Walk them through the Jones five-question citation framework (see `citation-guide` skill). Do not generate the citation automatically — coach them through building it themselves.
