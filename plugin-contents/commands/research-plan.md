Create a research plan following the Genealogical Proof Standard for: $ARGUMENTS

Load the `gps-methodology` and `record-types` skills for reference.

**Step 1 — Define the research question.**
Ask the researcher to state the specific question they want to answer. Do not accept vague goals like "learn about my ancestor." The question must be specific enough to have a definitive answer. Help them narrow it if needed.

Good examples: "Who were the parents of Peter Erb (1800–1867) of Earl Township, Lancaster County, Pennsylvania?" or "Did Jacob Allen serve in the Revolutionary War militia in Lancaster County?"

**Step 2 — Inventory existing research.**
Ask: "What do you already know about this person, and what sources have you already checked?" If the researcher has files in their connected Google Drive folder, scan the folder for relevant documents and summarize what exists.

**Step 3 — Identify the era and location.**
Based on the ancestor's time period and geographic location, use the `record-types` skill and `references/search-planning-by-era.md` to determine which record types could exist.

**Step 4 — Build the search plan.**
Create a prioritized checklist of record types to search, organized by:
- Most likely to answer the question (search these first)
- Supporting evidence (search these to corroborate)
- Context and background (search if time allows)

For each record type, note:
- What it might contain for this specific question
- Where to find it (specific repository or website)
- Whether the researcher has already checked it

**Step 5 — Create the tracking document.**
Write the research plan to a markdown file. Include:
- The specific research question
- What is already known (with sources)
- The prioritized search checklist with checkboxes
- A section for recording negative searches
- A section for recording conflicts found

Format as a working document the researcher will update as they search.

If the researcher has a connected Google Drive folder, save the plan there. Otherwise, provide it for them to save.
