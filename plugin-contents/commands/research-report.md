Generate a proof summary or research report for: $ARGUMENTS

Load the `gps-methodology` and `citation-guide` skills for reference.

**Before writing, verify GPS compliance.**
Check that all five GPS elements have been addressed:

1. ☐ Reasonably exhaustive search conducted (and documented)
2. ☐ All sources properly cited
3. ☐ Evidence analyzed and correlated
4. ☐ Conflicting evidence identified and resolved (or noted as unresolvable)
5. ☐ Conclusion follows logically from the evidence

If any element is missing or incomplete, tell the researcher what still needs to be done before a credible report can be written. Do not write the report until the evidence supports it.

**Step 1 — Gather the evidence.**
If the researcher has files in their connected Google Drive folder, scan for all documents related to the research question. If not, ask them to provide their evidence.

**Step 2 — Choose the format.**
Ask the researcher which format they need:

- **Proof summary**: A concise document (1–3 pages) that presents the conclusion, lists the evidence, and explains the reasoning. Used when evidence is straightforward and no serious conflicts exist.
- **Proof argument**: A longer, more detailed document that walks through complex or conflicting evidence step by step. Used when the conclusion requires resolving contradictions or relying on indirect/negative evidence.
- **Research report**: A comprehensive document that records everything searched (found and not found), all evidence collected, analysis performed, and conclusions reached. Used to document an entire research project.

**Step 3 — Write the document.**

**Proof Summary structure:**
1. Statement of the question
2. Summary of the conclusion
3. Evidence summary (list each source and what it contributes)
4. Reasoning (how the evidence supports the conclusion)
5. Source citations (full reference notes)

**Proof Argument structure:**
1. Statement of the question
2. Background (what was known before this research)
3. Sources searched (organized by record type)
4. Evidence analysis (source by source)
5. Correlation and conflict resolution
6. Conclusion with reasoning
7. Source citations (full reference notes)

**Research Report structure:**
1. Research objective
2. Known information at start of research
3. Research plan
4. Sources searched (including negative searches)
5. Findings by source
6. Analysis and correlation
7. Conflicts and resolutions
8. Conclusions
9. Recommendations for further research
10. Source citations

**Step 4 — Review for GPS compliance.**
After writing, review the document against all five GPS elements. Ensure:
- Every factual claim has a citation
- Every conflict is addressed
- The conclusion does not exceed what the evidence supports
- The reasoning is clear enough for another researcher to follow

Save the report to the researcher's Google Drive folder if connected, or provide it for download.
